---
title: Recording
description: Record Mighty camera, IMU, pose, and calibration data for replay or offline analysis.
---

Mighty can record ROS bag v1 files from the browser visualizer or directly on
the board. Use recordings to replay a run, debug VIO behavior, compare firmware
changes, or analyze sensor data offline.

## Record from the visualizer

The browser visualizer can record camera, IMU, and pose streams without a local
app:

1. Plug Mighty Camera into your machine.
2. Open [mightycamera.com/viz](https://mightycamera.com/viz) and wait for the
   board to connect.
3. Click **Start Recording**.
4. Move the board through the motion you want to capture.
5. Click **Stop Recording**.

The visualizer downloads a `.bag` file when recording stops. Keep the browser
tab open until the download starts.

## Record on the board

Mighty can also write a bag directly to its mounted SD card. Board-side
recording is useful when you do not want a browser to hold the recording session
open.

| State | Button action | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Idle / preview | Long press, about 2 seconds, then release | Start recording |
| Recording | Press and release | Stop recording |

Recording and VIO are mutually exclusive on the board. Stop VIO before starting
a board-side recording. If recording is already active, any button press stops
the recording.

Board-side full recordings are saved under `/mnt/sdcard` and include:

- `/cam0/image_raw/compressed`
- `/imu0`, IMU data with accelerometer and gyroscope measurements
- `/calib`, Calibration data for Camera and IMU

## LED states

Recording has the highest-priority LED pattern. When a recording is active, the
LED shows three quick flashes, then a pause.

| LED pattern | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Three quick flashes, pause | Recording is active |
| Slow blink | Idle / preview mode |
| Fast blink | VIO is starting or initializing |
| Solid on | VIO is tracking |
| Double pulse | VIO is degraded |
| Steady medium blink | VIO is lost |

If the LED stays in the idle pattern after a long press, recording did not
start. Common causes are an unmounted SD card, an existing active recording, or
VIO still running.
