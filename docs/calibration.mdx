Calibration estimates the camera intrinsics, the camera-to-IMU transform, and
the camera/IMU time offset. A good calibration improves VIO accuracy, reduces
drift, and makes recorded bags easier to replay and debug.

## Open the calibration page

1. Plug Mighty Camera into your machine.
2. Open [mightycamera.com/calib](https://mightycamera.com/calib).
3. Wait for the page to connect to the camera.

The first screen shows an AprilGrid calibration target made from many AprilTags.
This target is what the calibration server detects in the camera images.

## Measure the target

1. Put the browser in full screen so the AprilGrid is displayed at its final
   size.
2. Measure the black grid area from edge to edge, excluding the surrounding
   white margin.
3. Enter the measured width in centimeters.
4. Click **Next**.

Measure the physical target as it appears on your monitor. The calibration uses
this number to recover metric scale.

## Record a calibration bag

Point Mighty Camera at the AprilGrid and click **Start**. Move the camera
smoothly while keeping the grid visible and in focus.

Use varied motion:

- Rotate around all axes.
- Move forward and backward.
- Sweep the target through the center and edges of the fisheye view.
- Use slow figure-eight motions.
- Keep motion smooth enough to avoid blur.

The goal is to excite both the camera model and all IMU axes. It is fine if only
part of the grid is visible sometimes, but the calibration works best when the
target stays detectable for most of the recording.

Click **Stop** when you have captured enough motion, then click **Next**. The
page records a ROS bag. You can download it for debugging, but normal calibration
does not require downloading it.

## Run calibration

Click **Start Calibration**. The page uploads the recording and waits for the
calibration server to finish.

When calibration completes, open **Show Report** first. Check:

- Camera reprojection error is low, usually around or below 0.5 px for a good
  recording.
- IMU residuals are reasonable and do not show obvious timing or motion-blur
  failures.
- The estimated camera/IMU timeshift is small and stable for your hardware.

## Save to the device

If the report looks good, click **Save Calibration to Device**. This writes the
new `calib.yaml` to the board.

After saving, close the page and unplug or restart the device. The visualizer
and VIO runtime will use the saved calibration on the next run.

## Tips

- Use a bright, evenly lit screen.
- Avoid reflections, glare, and motion blur.
- Keep the target in focus.
- Do not cover the lens or shake the USB cable during recording.
- Recalibrate after changing the lens or mount.
